# Role taxonomy for perovskite PV interviews

## 1. Perovskite cell R&D engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Understand p-i-n and n-i-p stacks, transport layers, perovskite absorber composition, additives, passivation, and electrode choices.
- Interpret J-V, EQE, stabilized power output/MPPT, PL/TRPL, XRD, SEM/AFM, UPS/KPFM when relevant.
- Explain efficiency loss in Voc, Jsc, and FF terms and connect losses to recombination, resistance, optics, shunts, or morphology.
- Design controlled experiments with repeatability and device statistics.

## 2. Perovskite/silicon tandem engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Understand current matching, optical management, wide-bandgap perovskites, recombination/contact layers, TCO, sputter damage, and textured silicon compatibility.
- Distinguish two-terminal versus four-terminal tandems.
- Discuss EQE-integrated current, J-V consistency, stabilized efficiency, aperture area, certification, and subcell limitation.
- Explain route tradeoffs between spin coating, slot-die coating, evaporation, hybrid deposition, and process integration.

## 3. All-perovskite tandem engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Understand wide-bandgap and narrow-bandgap perovskites, Sn-Pb perovskite oxidation, recombination layers, current matching, and stability risks.
- Identify losses from low-bandgap subcells, voltage deficit, and interconnect layers.

## 4. Stability / reliability engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Design damp heat, thermal cycling, light soaking, MPP tracking, bias stress, UV, oxygen/moisture, and encapsulation experiments.
- Separate intrinsic material instability from packaging, electrode diffusion, ion migration, interface degradation, and measurement artifacts.
- Report test conditions precisely: temperature, humidity, illumination spectrum/intensity, bias/load, encapsulation, atmosphere, area, and failure criterion.

## 5. Encapsulation / module engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Understand barrier films/glass-glass packaging, edge sealants, lamination, interconnection, laser scribing, dead area, module aperture efficiency, moisture ingress, and thermal/mechanical stress.
- Diagnose module-level mismatch, shunting, delamination, corrosion, and edge degradation.

## 6. Process scale-up / manufacturing engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Translate lab recipes to coating/printing/evaporation process windows.
- Discuss uniformity, yield, takt time, solvent management, drying/crystallization, inline metrology, quality control, and statistical process control.
- Understand champion-device bias and the difference between best-cell efficiency and production yield.

## 7. Equipment engineer
Expected capabilities:
- Understand tool requirements for coating, drying/annealing, evaporation, sputtering, ALD, laser scribing, glovebox/dry-room integration, automation, and inline inspection.
- Link equipment parameters to film quality, throughput, uptime, contamination control, and maintenance.

## 8. Materials scientist
Expected capabilities:
- Connect precursor chemistry, additives, solvents, passivation molecules, transport materials, electrodes, and barrier materials to device performance and stability.
- Design materials screens with controls, compatibility checks, and degradation tests.

## 9. Senior technical lead / principal / manager
Expected capabilities:
- Make route-selection decisions balancing efficiency, stability, cost, manufacturability, supply chain, IP, and timeline.
- Build project milestones, risk registers, team roles, and go/no-go criteria.
- Explain failed projects with root-cause discipline and clear ownership.
