# Score Calibration Rubric

Use this reference to make scoring consistent across interviewers. Score only against the target role and seniority. If evidence is missing, mark it as not verified rather than assigning a high score.

## Scale definition

| Score | Meaning | General standard |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Weak / not demonstrated | Vague, mostly conceptual, cannot provide evidence or ownership |
| 2 | Below target | Some knowledge, but shallow, incomplete, or not role-relevant |
| 3 | Meets baseline | Competent for expected level; can explain examples and evidence |
| 4 | Strong | Deep hands-on experience, good judgment, clear evidence and tradeoffs |
| 5 | Outstanding | System-level understanding, independent ownership, strong evidence, scalable judgment |

## Calibrated dimensions

### Device physics and metrics

- 1: Knows PCE/Voc/Jsc/FF terms but cannot explain loss mechanisms.
- 3: Connects device metrics to recombination, transport, interfaces, EQE, and hysteresis.
- 5: Diagnoses performance changes quantitatively and links physics to process and product tradeoffs.

### Hands-on fabrication and process control

- 1: Describes recipes without knowing critical parameters.
- 3: Has personally operated relevant tools and can explain controls, repeatability, and parameter effects.
- 5: Defines process windows, DOE, scale-up constraints, yield impact, and transfer strategy.

### Characterization and data interpretation

- 1: Lists techniques without interpreting data.
- 3: Uses J-V/EQE/PL/TRPL/XRD/SEM/AFM/EIS/SCLC or similar tools to support claims.
- 5: Integrates multiple evidence streams to diagnose root causes and rule out artifacts.

### Stability and reliability

- 1: Says stability matters but cannot state protocol or mechanism.
- 3: Understands light/heat/humidity/bias tests, encapsulation status, MPPT/SPO, sample count, and degradation metrics.
- 5: Builds reliability strategy, separates failure mechanisms, and connects device, module, and qualification needs.

### Tandem understanding

- 1: Knows tandem means stacking cells, but cannot explain current matching.
- 3: Explains 2T/4T, current matching, bandgap, EQE integration, TCO/recombination layer basics.
- 5: Makes route decisions across optics, electrical integration, process compatibility, stability, and measurement validation.

### Scale-up and manufacturing judgment

- 1: Assumes small-area results transfer directly.
- 3: Discusses area, uniformity, defects, yield, throughput, process window, and module loss.
- 5: Owns pilot/manufacturing transfer logic with SPC, metrology, equipment limits, cost, and reliability tradeoffs.

### Communication and ownership

- 1: Overuses team claims, cannot define personal contribution.
- 3: Separates personal work, team work, evidence, and limitations clearly.
- 5: Communicates technical depth with concise structure, acknowledges uncertainty, and explains failures maturely.

## Hiring recommendation mapping

| Pattern | Suggested recommendation |
|---|---|
| Multiple 4-5 scores in role-critical areas and no major unresolved red flags | Strong recommend / recommend |
| Mostly 3 scores with one or two 4 scores and manageable gaps | Recommend / lean recommend |
| Mixed evidence, unclear ownership, or important claims unverified | Hold / lean recommend only after follow-up |
| Role-critical area below 3, or major exaggeration risk | Lean no / no |

Always state confidence level: high, medium, or low. Low confidence usually means the report is based on limited resume information or unverified interview notes.
