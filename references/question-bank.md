# Perovskite PV question bank

Use questions selectively based on role and seniority. Include follow-ups to test depth and ownership.

## Universal foundation

1. Explain the stack you worked on. What does each layer do?
   - Strong signals: clear layer functions, energy alignment, contact selectivity, recombination loss pathways.
   - Follow-ups: Why p-i-n instead of n-i-p? Which interface limited performance?

2. Your device efficiency improved. Which parameter improved: Voc, Jsc, or FF?
   - Strong signals: decomposes PCE improvement, shows statistics, identifies mechanism.
   - Follow-ups: Was the improvement shown in stabilized output or only reverse-scan J-V?

3. How do you confirm J-V results are reliable?
   - Strong signals: scan direction/rate, hysteresis, mask/aperture, EQE integration, MPPT/stabilized power, statistics.
   - Follow-ups: How many devices and batches? What was the distribution?

## Device physics and characterization

1. How would you diagnose low Voc?
   - Probe nonradiative recombination, quasi-Fermi level splitting, PL/TRPL, ideality factor, interface defects, band alignment.

2. How would you diagnose low FF?
   - Probe series resistance, shunt resistance, extraction barriers, transport layer conductivity, contact issues, ion migration, pinholes.

3. How would you use EQE data in tandem or single-junction analysis?
   - Probe integrated current, spectral mismatch, optical losses, parasitic absorption, current matching.

4. What does hysteresis indicate, and how do you reduce it?
   - Probe ion migration, traps, interfaces, scan protocol, passivation, transport layer choice.

## Film formation and process

1. Describe how your perovskite film forms during deposition and annealing.
   - Probe nucleation, crystal growth, solvent coordination, antisolvent/drying, additives, phase purity.

2. How would you transfer a spin-coated recipe to slot-die coating?
   - Probe wet film control, drying window, substrate temperature, meniscus stability, solvent system, coating speed, atmosphere, inline metrology.

3. What process variables most affect large-area uniformity?
   - Probe coating gap, flow rate, temperature, humidity, precursor aging, substrate cleanliness, annealing uniformity.

## Tandem-specific questions

1. In a two-terminal perovskite/silicon tandem, what limits current?
   - Probe subcell EQE, current matching, optical modeling, perovskite bandgap/thickness, silicon bottom-cell current.

2. How do you protect the perovskite during TCO sputtering?
   - Probe buffer layers, sputter power/pressure, damage mechanisms, ALD/evaporation interlayers, process temperature.

3. What evidence proves a reported tandem efficiency is credible?
   - Probe aperture area, certified measurement, EQE current balance, stabilized output, scan protocol, spectral correction.

4. How would you choose between 2T and 4T tandem routes?
   - Probe efficiency potential, process complexity, module integration, current matching sensitivity, optics, cost.

## Stability and reliability

1. Design a stability test for a perovskite module.
   - Probe MPP tracking, damp heat, thermal cycling, light soaking, encapsulation condition, failure criterion, controls.

2. A device degrades quickly under light but not in dark storage. What hypotheses do you test?
   - Probe ion migration, photo-induced phase segregation, interface reactions, electrode diffusion, heating, oxygen/moisture.

3. How do you separate intrinsic material degradation from encapsulation failure?
   - Probe control structures, calcium test/barrier data, edge-sealed vs unsealed, atmosphere comparison, spatial degradation mapping.

## Module and manufacturing

1. What changes when moving from 0.1 cm2 cells to mini-modules?
   - Probe uniformity, series resistance, scribing, dead area, shunts, aperture efficiency, yield.

2. How would you build a process window?
   - Probe DOE, critical parameters, inline metrics, acceptance criteria, SPC, batch-to-batch variation.

3. How do you define success for a pilot line?
   - Probe not only champion efficiency but yield, stability, throughput, reproducibility, cost, safety, uptime.

## Senior leadership

1. Which perovskite PV technology route would you prioritize and why?
   - Probe evidence-based tradeoffs, technical risk, milestones, resource planning, manufacturability, competitive positioning.

2. Describe a failed technical route and how you killed or redirected it.
   - Probe root-cause discipline, data thresholds, leadership judgment, learning extraction.
