# Resume Claim Verification

Use this reference whenever a resume, CV, LinkedIn-style profile, project summary, publication list, or interview note is provided. The goal is to convert claims into verification questions without assuming the claim is true.

## Evidence status labels

- **Explicit fact**: directly stated by the user, resume, JD, or notes.
- **Reasonable inference**: likely based on context, but not directly stated.
- **To verify**: must be confirmed in interview or follow-up.
- **Unsupported**: not enough information to use as evidence.

## Common claims and verification probes

| Resume claim | Verify by asking | Strong evidence | Red flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Achieved X% efficiency | Device area, mask area, scan direction, scan rate, stabilization, EQE current, average vs champion, certification | Area and protocol stated, statistics, MPPT/SPO, EQE match, independent validation if claimed | Only champion value, no area, no statistics, no steady-state data |
| Improved Voc/Jsc/FF/PCE | Which parameter changed and why? Controls? Number of devices? | Parameter-level analysis tied to mechanism and evidence | Improvement described as generic efficiency increase |
| Interface passivation | Which interface/defect? What material? Evidence? Side effects? | PL/TRPL/SCLC/EIS/XPS/KPFM/device statistics support mechanism | Cannot name defect/interface or controls |
| Tandem development | 2T or 4T? Top cell bandgap? Current matching? TCO/recombination layer? | EQE integration, limiting subcell identification, optical/electrical tradeoffs | Says tandem but only discusses single-junction perovskite |
| Stability improvement | Test conditions, encapsulation, MPPT, sample count, T80/T95, stress type | Clear protocol and failure analysis | Vague phrases like excellent stability or stable for many hours |
| Scale-up or module work | Substrate/module size, process method, uniformity, yield, interconnect, dead area | Area, yield, process window, defect map, module PCE | Only small-area spin coating despite scale-up claim |
| Led or owned project | Personal decisions, team size, deliverables, conflicts, failures | Specific ownership boundaries and decision examples | Uses team achievements without personal contribution detail |
| Published paper/patent | Exact contribution, experiments owned, corresponding data, writing role | Can explain figures, controls, reviewer concerns, limitations | Cannot explain own paper's key evidence |
| Equipment experience | Tool model/type, parameters controlled, maintenance/troubleshooting, safety | Specific tool-process link and failure cases | Only observed or followed SOP, but claims ownership |

## Verification workflow

1. Extract each major claim from the resume or notes.
2. Label it as explicit fact, inference, to verify, or unsupported.
3. Attach at least one primary verification question and 2-4 follow-up probes.
4. Identify what strong evidence would look like.
5. Identify red flags or evasive answers.
6. For hiring reports, include unresolved claims in the Items to verify section.
7. For candidate prep reports, convert unresolved claims into preparation tasks and honest answer boundaries.

## Do not overreach

Do not infer certified efficiency, device area, stability protocol, individual ownership, publication contribution, or manufacturing readiness unless explicitly provided.
