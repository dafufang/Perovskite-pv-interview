# Follow-Up Question Trees

Use these trees to keep technical interviews deep and adaptive. Start broad, then drill down only as far as the candidate's background and target level justify.

## Interface passivation / 界面钝化

1. Which interface was targeted: perovskite/ETL, perovskite/HTL, grain boundary, surface, or buried interface?
2. What defect or loss mechanism did you believe you were addressing?
3. What material or treatment did you use, and why?
4. What controls did you run?
5. Which metrics improved: Voc, FF, hysteresis, stability, PL lifetime, nonradiative loss?
6. What characterization supports the mechanism: PL, TRPL, SCLC, EIS, XPS, UPS, KPFM, GIWAXS, ToF-SIMS?
7. Did the treatment create side effects: transport barrier, ion migration, moisture sensitivity, thermal instability?
8. Did it work statistically and at larger area?

## Thin-film crystallization / 薄膜结晶

1. What deposition method and substrate size were used?
2. What parameter controlled nucleation/growth: solvent, antisolvent, additive, temperature, humidity, gas flow, evaporation rate?
3. How did morphology change: grain size, pinholes, roughness, phase purity, orientation?
4. What evidence supports this: SEM/AFM/XRD/GIWAXS/PL mapping?
5. How did device metrics change and why?
6. What was the process window and failure mode outside the window?
7. Could the method scale beyond spin coating or small area?

## Tandem cell experience / 叠层电池经验

1. Was it 2T, 4T, or mechanically stacked?
2. What were the top and bottom cell technologies and target bandgaps?
3. Which subcell limited current and how did you prove it?
4. How did EQE integrated current compare with J-V current?
5. What transparent electrode or recombination/interconnection layer was used?
6. How did you reduce parasitic absorption and sputter/process damage?
7. How did top-cell thickness, bandgap, and optical design affect Jsc/Voc/FF?
8. What measurement artifacts or certification issues did you consider?

## Stability and degradation / 稳定性与退化

1. What stress was applied: light, heat, humidity, oxygen, bias, UV, thermal cycling?
2. Was the device encapsulated? What was the encapsulation stack?
3. Was testing under MPPT/SPO, open-circuit, short-circuit, or periodic J-V?
4. What were the conditions: temperature, RH, intensity, spectrum, atmosphere, duration, sample count?
5. What was the degradation metric: T80/T95, PCE retention, Voc/Jsc/FF loss?
6. What failure mode was identified and with which evidence?
7. Was there reversible degradation or burn-in?
8. How would you design the next experiment to separate moisture, ion migration, and interface degradation?

## Scale-up and manufacturing / 放大与量产

1. What was the starting area and target area?
2. Which process step became unstable during scale-up?
3. What metrics were tracked: uniformity, yield, defect density, throughput, line speed, scrap?
4. What process window did you define?
5. How did you detect and classify defects?
6. What in-line or off-line metrology did you use?
7. How did encapsulation, interconnection, laser scribing, or TCO affect module performance?
8. What tradeoff did you make between efficiency, stability, cost, and manufacturability?

## Project ownership / 个人贡献

1. What exactly was your role in the project?
2. Which decision did you personally make?
3. Which data or figures did you generate?
4. What did teammates or collaborators contribute?
5. What failed, and what did you change after the failure?
6. What would you do differently now?
