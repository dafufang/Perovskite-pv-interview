# Role Levels and Interview Depth

Use this reference to calibrate question difficulty, expected evidence, and scoring by seniority.

## Level map

| Level | Typical profile | Interview emphasis | Expected evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intern / fresh graduate | Course, thesis, early lab experience | Fundamentals, learning ability, basic experiment discipline | Clear concepts, honest ownership, ability to explain one project accurately |
| Master's / PhD candidate | Research-focused, paper or thesis experience | Mechanism, experimental design, data interpretation | Controls, repeatability, characterization, contribution to publications or projects |
| 1-3 years engineer | Early industrial or postdoc experience | Hands-on execution, troubleshooting, reproducibility | Device/process data, actual tools used, failure analysis examples |
| 3-7 years engineer | Independent contributor | Problem ownership, process optimization, cross-functional work | Statistics, process windows, stability data, yield or scale-up examples |
| Senior / staff / principal | Technical owner | Route judgment, architecture decisions, mentoring, platform building | Tradeoff analysis, roadmap thinking, transfer from R&D to pilot or manufacturing |
| Manager / director / technical lead | Team or program leader | Strategy, resource allocation, risk management, hiring and execution | Team outcomes, decision records, IP/competitor view, milestone delivery |

## Difficulty calibration

- For junior candidates, do not over-penalize lack of industrial scale-up, but verify fundamentals and honest ownership.
- For mid-level candidates, require concrete examples of troubleshooting, controls, repeatability, and data-driven optimization.
- For senior candidates, prioritize route selection, system tradeoffs, team influence, and ability to identify hidden risks.
- For leadership candidates, include commercialization, cost, yield, reliability, IP, partner/vendor, and team-building questions.

## Evidence expectations by level

| Capability | Junior signal | Mid-level signal | Senior/lead signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Device physics | Explains Voc/Jsc/FF/PCE and recombination basics | Uses PL/TRPL/EQE/SCLC/EIS to diagnose performance | Connects physics to process windows and product metrics |
| Fabrication | Knows process flow and precautions | Personally operated coating/evaporation/sputtering/ALD or line tools | Defines scalable process route and controls variability |
| Stability | Knows heat/light/humidity stress matters | Designs MPPT or ISOS-style tests and interprets degradation | Builds reliability strategy linking encapsulation, interfaces, and qualification |
| Tandem | Explains current matching and bandgap basics | Handles TCO/recombination layer/EQE/J-V consistency | Chooses 2T/4T route and manages optical/electrical/manufacturing tradeoffs |
| Scale-up | Understands small-area vs module difference | Discusses uniformity, defects, yield, coating window | Owns pilot-line transfer, process control, throughput, and cost risk |
