# Perovskite PV Role Profiles

Use this reference to map a resume or job description to role-specific expectations. Select only the profiles relevant to the user's request.

## Perovskite single-junction R&D engineer

- Core mission: improve single-junction perovskite device efficiency, reproducibility, and stability.
- Must-have skills: p-i-n or n-i-p device stack, thin-film crystallization, interface engineering, passivation, J-V/EQE/PL/TRPL/XRD/SEM interpretation.
- Add-ons: statistical device analysis, MPPT, degradation analysis, scalable coating or evaporation.
- Key probes: device area, scan protocol, steady-state output, champion vs average efficiency, control experiments.
- Mismatch signals: only recipe following, no mechanism, no statistical repeatability, cannot explain parameter changes.

## Perovskite/silicon tandem engineer

- Core mission: integrate perovskite top cell with silicon bottom cell for high-efficiency tandem devices or modules.
- Must-have skills: current matching, optical management, wide-bandgap perovskites, transparent electrodes, recombination/interconnection layer, EQE integration, 2T/4T distinction.
- Add-ons: textured silicon compatibility, sputter damage mitigation, TCO optimization, module integration.
- Key probes: limiting sub-cell, bandgap choice, top-cell thickness, parasitic absorption, J-V vs EQE current consistency, certified vs lab efficiency.
- Mismatch signals: claims tandem experience but cannot explain current matching, TCO, recombination layer, or measurement artifacts.

## All-perovskite tandem engineer

- Core mission: develop wide-bandgap and narrow-bandgap perovskite subcells and connect them in tandem.
- Must-have skills: Sn/Pb narrow-bandgap perovskites, oxidation control, bandgap tuning, interconnection layer, stability tradeoffs.
- Add-ons: low-temperature processing, solvent orthogonality, encapsulation of Sn-containing devices.
- Key probes: Sn oxidation mitigation, subcell current balance, recombination layer, EQE integration, stability bottleneck.
- Mismatch signals: only single-junction experience presented as tandem, no narrow-bandgap or interconnect knowledge.

## Stability and reliability engineer

- Core mission: understand, test, and improve device/module stability under light, heat, humidity, oxygen, bias, and mechanical stress.
- Must-have skills: MPPT, damp heat, thermal cycling, UV/light soaking, encapsulated vs unencapsulated testing, degradation mechanism analysis.
- Add-ons: ISOS-style protocols, IEC-style reliability awareness, failure analysis with PL/EL/XRD/XPS/ToF-SIMS/EIS.
- Key probes: test conditions, initial burn-in, T80/T95, encapsulation status, sample size, degradation mode, recovery behavior.
- Mismatch signals: says stable but lacks test conditions, no sample size, no device architecture, no mechanism.

## Encapsulation and module engineer

- Core mission: protect perovskite devices/modules and manage interconnect, barrier, edge seal, and reliability.
- Must-have skills: barrier films/glass-glass, edge seal, WVTR concept, lamination constraints, interconnection, module layout, moisture/thermal/mechanical failure.
- Add-ons: accelerated reliability tests, scale-up to mini-module/module, production-compatible encapsulants.
- Key probes: encapsulation stack, process temperature, adhesive compatibility, leakage paths, degradation after damp heat/thermal cycling.
- Mismatch signals: treats encapsulation as packaging only, cannot link materials/process to degradation.

## Thin-film/process engineer

- Core mission: develop controllable, scalable deposition and treatment processes.
- Must-have skills: slot-die, blade coating, spin coating, evaporation, sputtering, ALD, annealing, solvent/additive control, uniformity and defect analysis.
- Add-ons: process window design, in-line metrology, DOE, SPC, yield improvement.
- Key probes: coating window, film thickness/uniformity, defect density, substrate size, throughput, environmental control.
- Mismatch signals: only small-area spin coating experience for a scale-up role, no process control language.

## Equipment engineer

- Core mission: select, integrate, tune, and maintain equipment for perovskite cell/module R&D or manufacturing.
- Must-have skills: vacuum/coating/sputter/ALD/laser/interconnection equipment, process-tool interaction, maintenance and troubleshooting.
- Add-ons: supplier qualification, automation, in-line metrology, safety and uptime metrics.
- Key probes: tool parameters, failure modes, maintenance routines, process transfer, equipment capability limits.
- Mismatch signals: cannot distinguish equipment operation from process ownership.

## Materials R&D engineer

- Core mission: design or screen perovskite compositions, transport layers, additives, passivators, electrodes, encapsulants, or barrier materials.
- Must-have skills: material-property-device link, chemical stability, energy levels, defect chemistry, compatibility with process route.
- Add-ons: synthesis, formulation, high-throughput screening, supplier qualification.
- Key probes: mechanism of additive/passivator, side effects, concentration window, evidence from spectroscopy/device statistics.
- Mismatch signals: additive shopping without mechanism or controls.

## Technical lead / project owner

- Core mission: define technical route, organize team, manage milestones, and move technology toward product or pilot line.
- Must-have skills: route comparison, technical roadmap, risk register, cross-functional coordination, hiring, IP/competitor awareness.
- Add-ons: pilot-line transfer, external collaboration, customer or investor communication.
- Key probes: why this route, kill criteria, resource allocation, milestone design, failure lessons, team contribution vs personal contribution.
- Mismatch signals: only individual-contributor detail, no route-level tradeoff or execution discipline.
